Totalitarian States




What is Totalitarian States?

Totalitarians are dictators who have total control over the lives of the people living within the state.




What is a Ideoligie?

When beliefs are identified as a common view and powerful enough to guide
common people's behavior, it comes to an ideology. Ideology guides the behavior
of individuals, groups, governments (and their decisions). A non-government
organization (I.e.) Amnesty International works to free political prisoners.

Cult of personality:

A government's attempt to build an exaggerated and heroic image of a
leader—often through constant praising—leads to the development of a
personality cult.

Fear, Repression and terror

Totalitarian nations are often managed as police states, with a high level of
monitoring of the people living there and harsh punishments for those who break
the rules and those for who step out of line.


Propaganda and censorship:

Propoganda is the spreading of ideas, information, or rumor for the
purpose of helping an institution, a cause, or a person

State controlled economic policies:

This feature is referred to have a powerful and strong governmental control over the
economic development of the country, reducing economic freedoms.
